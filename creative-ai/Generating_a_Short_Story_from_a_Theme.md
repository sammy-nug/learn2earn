# 🎯 Objective: Practice Creative Writing Through Prompts

---

## 1️⃣ Choose a theme (e.g., "friendship," "space exploration," "overcoming fear").

## 2️⃣ Write a simple generative prompt: "Write a short story about [theme] in under 300 words."

> Write a short story about **[theme]** in under 300 words.

This prompt:
- Defines the task (short story)
- Sets a length constraint (under 300 words)
- Leaves creative freedom for tone, setting, and characters

---

## Test with at least two different themes and compare the style of outputs.


# Test Case 1: Theme — Friendship

### Prompt
> Write a short story about friendship in under 300 words.

### Output

Lena met Aisha on the first day of school, when both reached for the same fallen notebook. They laughed, a little embarrassed, and decided to share the desk instead.  

At first, their friendship was built on small things—shared snacks, whispered jokes, and complaints about homework. But when Lena’s father lost his job, the laughter faded. Lena grew quiet, skipping lunch and avoiding everyone.

Aisha noticed.

One afternoon, she showed up at Lena’s house with two cups of instant noodles and a stack of old comics. They sat on the floor, reading and slurping in comfortable silence. Aisha didn’t ask questions. She didn’t offer advice. She just stayed.

Weeks passed. Things slowly improved at Lena’s home. But what Lena remembered most wasn’t the worry or the fear—it was the quiet loyalty of someone who chose to remain.

Years later, at graduation, Lena squeezed Aisha’s hand and whispered, “Thank you for staying.”

Aisha smiled. “That’s what friends do.”

# Test Case 2: Theme — Space Exploration

### Prompt
> Write a short story about space exploration in under 300 words.

### Output

Commander Imani floated toward the observation window as Earth shrank into a pale blue marble. The silence of space wasn’t empty—it hummed with possibility.

The mission was simple on paper: survey Kepler-442b for signs of life. But after seven years in cryosleep and months of travel, “simple” felt like a fragile word.

When the scanners detected oxygen fluctuations, the crew held their breath. Imani guided the lander through violet clouds, descending onto terrain that shimmered like glass.

She stepped onto the surface first.

The ground pulsed faintly beneath her boots. Strange, bioluminescent plants bent toward her as if curious. Then her radio crackled.

“Commander,” her co-pilot whispered, “the atmosphere readings are stable.”

Imani removed her helmet.

The air was cool and sharp. She inhaled slowly, feeling the weight of history press against her chest. Humanity had crossed the dark and found something that breathed back.

As she planted the mission flag, she didn’t feel like a conqueror. She felt like a guest.

Above her, unfamiliar stars burned brightly—proof that the universe was vast, but not unreachable.

# Comparison of Styles

| Aspect | Friendship Story | Space Exploration Story |
|---------|------------------|-------------------------|
| Tone | Warm, intimate, emotional | Awe-filled, expansive, adventurous |
| Setting | Everyday, relatable | Futuristic, cosmic |
| Focus | Emotional connection | Discovery and wonder |
| Atmosphere | Quiet, personal | Vast, exploratory |
| Conflict Type | Emotional hardship | Scientific and environmental uncertainty |
