# 🎯 Objective: Strengthen Weak Prompts Using Patterns

---

## 1️⃣ Take a vague or under-specified prompt (e.g., "Write about climate change.").

**Weak / Under-Specified Prompt**
> Write about climate change.  

---

## 2️⃣ Transform it using instructional or comparative patterns:
**Instructional Pattern**

> Write a 200-word explanation of climate change for high school learners.

- Specifies word count (200 words)  
- Defines audience (high school learners)  
 

---

## 3️⃣ Comparative:

> Compare the effects of climate change on coastal vs inland regions in 5 bullet points.

- Clearly defines comparison task  
- Specifies format    

---

# 📊 Documented Improvement in Clarity and Usefulness

| Aspect | Weak Prompt | Instructional Pattern | Comparative Pattern |
|--------|------------|----------------------|--------------------|
| Audience Defined | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Length Controlled | ❌ | ✅ | Implicit (5 bullets) |
| Structure Specified | ❌ | Partial | ✅ |
| Focus Narrowed | ❌ | Moderate | Strong |
| Evaluation Ease | Low | Medium | High |

---

# ✅ Conclusion

Transforming vague prompts into structured instructional or comparative patterns:

- Increases clarity  
- Reduces ambiguity  
- Improves output consistency  
- Makes results easier to evaluate  
- Enhances practical usefulness  

Structured prompting leads to more predictable, relevant, and high-quality responses.
